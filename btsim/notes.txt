Currently the plan tree is build once when the wallet first has UTXOs and POs.
The wallets pick the best branch they can from the tree. And commit to it.

They have no preferences over UTXOs on the orderbook. They should take a look at the utxos and 
recompute scores on the tree. Perhaps a sparse branch becomes dense and the wallet should reconsider the branch.

They should always keep track of their best branch (best worst case -- interms of external penalties).

There are lot of optimizations to look into: 
- caching scores for leaves
- depending on the privacy metrics used other things may be memoized. Subsums, combinations, etc. Denominations, etc.

We also need to recompute when there are any externalities. Until we cross a threshold where no matter what external events
happen we always happy with the branch that we have. 

Currently there is a lot of boilerplate and messy code in actions.rs. We need to step back and carefully refactor the layers of 
abstraction correctly. 

There is a strategies which define what actions are even available to a wallet. 
These need to get refined or ignored where there is a plan tree.
